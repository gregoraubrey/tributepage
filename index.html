Vasily Arkhipov

The man who saved the world

The life and legacy of Vasily Arkhipov:

  • 1926 - Born in Staraya Kupavna, near Moscow.
  • 1945 - Served in the Soviet-Japanese War on a minesweeper.
  • 1947 - Graduated from the Caspian Higher Naval School.
  • 1961 - Made the deputy commander of the submarine K-19.
  • 1962 - United States Navy destroyers drop depth charges on a Russian submarine in international waters. Arkhipov is the only one of 3 officers who does not agree to launch a nuclear torpedo, effectively avoiding nuclear war with the United States.
  • 1975 - Promoted to rear admiral, becoming head of the Kirov Naval Academy.
  • 1981 - Promoted once more to vice admiral, before retiring a few years later.
  • 1998 - Died at the age of 72 in Zheleznodorozhny.
  • 2002 - Described by Thomas S. Blanton as being "the man who saved the world".
  • 2017 - Posthumously awarded the first ever Future of Life Award.
